## Section A — Technical Reference Document (1 entry, expected: 1 node)

A single coherent document. Should commit as one node, not be exploded into the bullet hierarchy. Tests structured-document recognition.

---

Creating a decentralized, user-controlled social network without relying on central servers or algorithms is feasible with modern tools, even for those with limited programming experience. The architecture has several layers: decentralized data storage (Gun.js for real-time peer-to-peer syncing, Hypercore for built-in synchronization, OrbitDB on IPFS for content like images), communication protocols (Matrix Protocol for federated messaging with end-to-end encryption, Secure Scuttlebutt for offline-first peer-to-peer sharing via "feeds"), frontend tools (low-code options like Bubble or Glide, or Vue/React with Tailwind for custom UIs), and progressive web app distribution via Ionic or Workbox to bypass app stores. User-defined rules can be expressed visually through Blockly or Node-RED. Distribution via Electron for desktop and Capacitor for native mobile. Security through Libsodium and TweetNaCl for encryption, with Solid Project as an option for personal data pods. Existing platforms like Manyverse (built on Scuttlebutt) and Element (Matrix client) can be forked rather than reinvented. Key challenges: data persistence in P2P networks requires peer relay servers; user education needs serious onboarding for managing rules and connections; abuse moderation without central oversight depends on reputation systems. A reasonable starting stack: Vue.js + Ionic for the PWA, Gun.js + IPFS for backend, Blockly + custom JavaScript for the rules engine, deployed as a PWA and packaged via Capacitor for mobile.

## Section D — Recipes (expected: 6 nodes)

Chicken Tacos. Boneless skinless chicken thighs (1-2 lbs), seasoned with onion powder, minced garlic, chili powder, cumin, chipotle chili pepper, salt and pepper. Sear in canola oil with chopped white onion, then transfer to 425°F oven for 25 minutes. Shred with two forks. Pico de gallo: finely diced white onion, minced cilantro, halved grape tomatoes, lime, salt and pepper. Crema: Greek yogurt whisked with lime juice and salt. Serve with crumbled queso fresco, fried corn tortillas, lime wedges, Valentina hot sauce.

---

Slow Roasted Plum Tomatoes. 1.5 lbs plum tomatoes (about 6 large), 3 tbsp olive oil, dried thyme, head of garlic peeled and smashed, kosher salt, 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar. Halve tomatoes cut-side up in pyrex pan, drizzle with oil, scatter garlic, sprinkle thyme and salt. 325°F for 25 minutes, then reduce to 200°F for 2 hours. Drizzle balsamic after one hour. Baste occasionally.

---

Roasted Lemongrass Chicken with Sweet Lime Sauce. Chicken pieces marinated with finely chopped lemongrass, onion, garlic, galangal/ginger, fresh red chilies, fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, and thick coconut milk. Refrigerate 30 minutes to 6 hours. Roast at 350°F covered for 45 minutes, uncovered another 20 until golden-brown. Side sauce: water, lime juice, rice vinegar, galangal, garlic, fish sauce, honey, cornstarch slurry to thicken. Serve with Thai jasmine rice.

---

Summer Lemon Shrimp Pasta. One stick Kerrygold butter, 2 tbsp minced garlic, white onion sliced lengthwise, 1 cup dry white wine, 1 cup shredded Parmesan, 1 tbsp white wine vinegar, 1 cup lemon juice (divided), linguine, 2 lbs cooked small shrimp. Melt butter, cook onions until semi-translucent, add garlic until aromatic. Add vinegar and wine, reduce 5-7 min. Add half the lemon juice, simmer 4 min. Add shrimp, cook 4 min more. Salt and pepper. Toss with cooked linguine. Optional red pepper flakes for spice. Serves 4-5.

Sweetgreen Harvest Bowl knockoff. One sweet potato chopped, 3-4 cups Lacinato kale, 1.5-2 cups cooked wild rice, 1 cup cooked chicken (rotisserie works), 1 chopped apple, 4 oz goat cheese, 1/2 cup slivered almonds. Dressing: 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar, 2 tbsp honey dijon mustard, 1/2 cup olive oil, salt, pepper. Roast sweet potato halves at 400°F for 40 min. Cook wild rice per package. Combine.

Chicken Mole (grandma's recipe, 7 servings). Boil chicken in 16 cups water with half onion, 2 bay leaves, 2 garlic cloves for 25-30 min. Reserve 9 cups broth. Add Mexican chocolate disk to broth. Toast guajillo, ancho, pasilla chiles, sesame seeds, cinnamon stick, allspice, cloves, cumin, garlic, onion, saltine crackers, roma tomato — add each to blender as toasted. Add chiles from broth. Add Doña Maria mole jar. Blend smooth. Heat in oiled pot until thickened, add chicken.
